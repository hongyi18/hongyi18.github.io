### GR

- Dirac, [General Theory of Relativity](http://www.geocities.jp/nomonomo2007/ReadingPhysics/Dirac/Dirac_GeneralTheoryRelativity.pdf). (GR can be learned in just one week from a completely algebraic approach!)
- James Hartle, [Gravity: An Introduction to Einstein’s General Relativity](https://link.zhihu.com/?target=http%3A//web.physics.ucsb.edu/~hartle/gravity.html). (Including more physical intuition than math formulas.)
- Sean Carroll, [Spacetime and Geometry: An Introduction to General Relativity](https://www.preposterousuniverse.com/spacetimeandgeometry/). (Formulating GR in a more geometrical approach.)
- Robert Wald, [General Relativity](https://cdn.preterhuman.net/texts/science_and_technology/physics/General_Relativity_Theory/General Relativity - R. Wald.pdf).

### QFT

- David Tong, [lecture note](http://www.damtp.cam.ac.uk/user/tong/qft.html). (The one for canonical quantization.)
- Mark Srednicki, [Quantum Field Theory](https://web.physics.ucsb.edu/~mark/qft.html). (The one for path integral.)
- Steven Weinberg, The Quantum Theory of Fields, Volume [I](https://cloudflare-ipfs.com/ipfs/bafykbzaceak6maaye64o5k3dcabsxjwld7s34mkcblhraigu5ogi57zbovu72?filename=Steven%20Weinberg%20-%20The%20Quantum%20Theory%20of%20Fields%20%28Volume%201%29-Cambridge%20University%20Press%20%281995%29.pdf) and [II](https://cloudflare-ipfs.com/ipfs/bafykbzacedg4bsys6p3k5bcqs6qwc4aja7tuy6piufemrpz4t47ok2y3j5ue2?filename=Steven%20Weinberg%20-%20The%20Quantum%20Theory%20of%20Fields%2C%20Vol.%202_%20Modern%20Applications-Cambridge%20University%20Press%20%281996%29.pdf). (The one every theorist should read.) Solutions of selected problems can be found in [The Quantum Theory of Fields: Vol. I - Ray Hagimoto](https://rayhagimoto.xyz/post/steven-weinberg-quantum-theory-of-fields-vol-i-solutions-to-selected-problems/).
- Matthew D. Schwartz, [Quantum Field Theory and the Standard Model](https://schwartzqft.fas.harvard.edu/).
- Howard Georgi, [Lie Algebras in Particle Physics](http://mural.uv.es/rusanra/Lie Algebras in Particle Physics 2ª ed - From Isospin to Unified Theories (Georgi, 1999).pdf).
